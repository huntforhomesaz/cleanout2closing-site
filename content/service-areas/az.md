## Step 1 — Understand how Cleanout2Closing fits in Arizona

Cleanout2Closing is built around a simple idea: most estate properties need a blend of on-the-ground coordination and a thoughtful sale, not just a sign in the yard.

In Arizona, that often means:

- Heirs or trustees are not nearby, or they are juggling work and family.  
- The property has years of belongings, outbuildings, or deferred maintenance.  
- There may be attorneys, fiduciaries, or multiple decision makers involved.  

My role is to be the person who:

- Walks the property in person.  
- Helps you figure out what needs to happen with contents and vendors.  
- Coordinates cleanout, repairs, and preparation with qualified vendors.  
- Guides pricing, marketing, negotiations, and escrow as a licensed Realtor.

The reason I start in Tucson is simple. It is where I can promise that level of involvement without stretching too thin.

## Step 3 — If your property is in Arizona but not near Tucson

There are situations where the property is elsewhere in Arizona, but my approach is still useful.

Examples:

- A trust property in another Arizona city where the decision makers live out of state.  
- A family cabin or second home that has effectively become an estate property.  
- A situation where multiple heirs live in different cities and want one person to coordinate the property.

In those cases, I look at three things before I say yes:

1. How much on-site coordination is required.  
2. How many rounds of vendor work and visits will be needed.  
3. Whether I can realistically stay personally accountable from start to finish.

If the honest answer is that someone closer should lead the project, I will tell you that. In many cases, I can still:

- Help you think through priorities and timelines.  
- Share what has worked well in similar Tucson cases.  
- Suggest what kind of Realtor or vendor team to look for in your city.

You do not have to guess alone, even if we decide I am not the best fit to take the lead.

## Step 5 — A quick note about legal, tax, and labor boundaries

Because this page speaks to Arizona as a whole, it is important to be clear about what I do and do not do.

- I am a licensed Realtor, not an attorney, accountant, or tax professional.  
- I do not provide legal, accounting, or tax advice, and I do not act as an executor or fiduciary.  
- My role is to coordinate property-related tasks and the sale alongside your attorney or fiduciary when they are involved.  

On the labor side:

- I do not run a junk removal crew, moving company, or cleaning company.  
- I coordinate qualified vendors, schedule and oversee their work where appropriate, and help you keep estimates, invoices, and approvals organized.

If you ever need input that crosses into legal or tax advice, I will encourage you to talk with the right professional in Arizona and work with them as part of the team.
