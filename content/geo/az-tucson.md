## Step 1 — Understand what makes Tucson estates a little different

Tucson has its own mix of factors that show up in estate and senior transition properties:

- A growing share of the population is 65 and older, with many retirees choosing Tucson for climate and affordability. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  
- There are many 55 plus communities and snowbird style properties that sit empty part of the year. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  
- Older block and brick homes, flat roofs, and desert landscaping often reflect years of sun and deferred maintenance.  
- Sheds, workshops, and garages can be full of tools, equipment, and stored items that take time to sort.  

When a parent, grandparent, or relative passes away or needs to move, families are left with:

- A house full of belongings.  
- Questions about what to keep, donate, sell, or haul away.  
- Decisions about repairs and updates that make sense for the local market.  
- Pressure to get the property sold without burning the family out.

My role is to be the local person who sees the house in context, coordinates what needs to happen, and guides you from cleanout to closing in a way that fits Tucson, not a generic checklist.

## Step 2 — What I handle in and around Tucson

Here is what I typically handle when a Tucson area property needs more than just a simple listing:

- Walk the property or arrange a detailed virtual walkthrough.  
- Help you think through contents: what to keep, donate, sell, or discard.  
- Coordinate junk removal, donation pickups, and cleanout vendors.  
- Line up cleaners, landscapers, and trades for realistic repairs or touch ups.  
- Advise on price and strategy for the Tucson market, based on your goals.  
- Manage showings, offers, negotiations, and escrow as a licensed Realtor.  
- Keep heirs, trustees, and professionals updated with clear summaries.

I stay in my lane on purpose:

- I do not run a junk removal crew or moving company.  
- I coordinate and oversee independent vendors so you are not juggling them yourself.  
- I am not an attorney, accountant, or tax professional.  
- I do not act as executor or fiduciary.  
- Your attorney or fiduciary handles court, probate, trust, and legal paperwork.

My value is in coordinating the property, vendors, and sale so the Tucson part moves in an organized way while you and your professionals handle the legal and financial side.

## Step 5 — A quick note about legal, tax, and labor boundaries

Because this page speaks to Tucson as a hub, it is important to be clear about boundaries.

- I am a licensed Realtor in Arizona, not an attorney, accountant, or tax professional.  
- I do not provide legal or tax advice and I do not act as executor or fiduciary.  
- My work sits alongside your attorney, fiduciary, or other professionals, not in their place.  

On the labor and vendor side:

- I do not operate a junk removal crew, moving company, or daily care service.  
- I coordinate and oversee independent vendors as part of preparing the property and managing the sale.  
- You or the estate pay vendors directly, and I help keep estimates, invoices, and approvals organized so everyone stays on the same page.

If a question crosses into legal or tax territory, I will encourage you to speak with the right professional and then work with them on the property side.
