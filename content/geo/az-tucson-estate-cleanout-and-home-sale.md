## How the process works here in Tucson

The steps for an inherited or probate house are similar across situations, but Tucson adds its own realities, from flat roofs and desert landscaping to snowbird style second homes.

Here is how I typically guide families through it.

### Step 1: Clarify the situation and decision makers

We start with a conversation about:

- Who currently has authority to make decisions  
- Whether an attorney or fiduciary is involved  
- Where you live and how available you can be  

I ask for a simple overview of the property, timelines, and any court or family pressures you already know about. If you need legal or tax guidance, I will encourage you to talk with the right professional before major decisions are made.

### Step 2: Document the property

Next, we get a clear picture of the property itself.

- In person walkthrough if someone can meet me at the house  
- Or a combination of lockbox access, photos, and video if no one is local  

We pay attention to:

- Contents and belongings  
- Roof, mechanicals, and obvious safety issues  
- Landscaping, outbuildings, and any storage  
- Signs of water, pests, or long periods of vacancy  

This becomes the baseline for cleanout, vendor planning, and pricing in the Tucson market.

### Step 3: Build a contents and cleanout plan

Together we outline a realistic plan for what happens with belongings.

- What must be kept or shipped to family  
- What can be donated or sold locally  
- What should be hauled away  

I bring in trusted junk removal and cleanout vendors, and when it makes sense, estate sale or buyout options. You approve estimates before anyone is scheduled, and vendors are paid directly by you or the estate.

### Step 4: Coordinate vendors and preparation

Once the cleanout plan is set, we sequence the work.

- Cleanout and donation pickups  
- Cleaning and yard work  
- Targeted repairs that actually matter to Tucson buyers  

I coordinate scheduling, keep you updated, and confirm completion with photos and simple summaries. The goal is to get the property ready for market without over improving or wasting money on work that will not change the outcome.

### Step 5: Guide pricing, listing, and offers

With the house ready, we talk about price and strategy in plain language.

- Current comparable sales in Tucson and nearby areas  
- Buyer expectations for that part of town and price range  
- Options like selling as is, doing light improvements, or in rare cases considering an investor offer  

I list the property, manage showings, handle offers, and negotiate on your behalf. You stay in control of decisions, but you do not have to manage the day to day communication.

### Step 6: Escrow and hand off

Once you accept an offer, I help shepherd the property side of escrow:

- Inspections and access for appraisers and contractors  
- Negotiation of repair requests related to the property  
- Status updates for you and any professionals involved  

Your attorney or fiduciary handles court and legal steps. I keep everyone aligned on the property and sale until the transaction closes and the house is no longer on your plate.

## Common Tucson questions about inherited houses

### What should I do first with an inherited house in Tucson

Make sure you understand who has legal authority and what the estate requires, then get a clear picture of the property and your timelines. A short call is usually enough for me to outline next steps and help you avoid rushing into choices you cannot unwind.

### Can you handle cleanout and sale if I live out of state

Yes. Many of my clients never set foot in the house during the process. The key is good communication and clarity up front about decisions, timelines, and how we will handle access to the property.

### Do you buy houses for cash or only list them

My focus is on coordinating cleanout and full market sales. I can help you compare an investor offer to a traditional listing and connect you with investors when that is worth exploring, but I do not position myself as the cash buyer.

### Are you a junk removal company

No. I coordinate junk removal and other vendors instead of running a crew myself. That way you have one accountable person for the whole property and sale, while specialists handle the physical work.
