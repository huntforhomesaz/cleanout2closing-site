## Why out-of-state ownership is so common in Green Valley

Green Valley is an unincorporated retirement community in Pima County, developed around multiple homeowner associations and recreation facilities geared to 55+ living.   

Key realities that matter for you:

- Median age is about 73.3 years, almost double the Arizona state median.   
- Many neighborhoods are age-restricted, with very few households that include children.   
- A high share of homes are owned by retirees, snowbirds, or out-of-state families.  
- It is common for heirs or trustees to live in other states and to rely on local help for cleanout and sale.

On top of that, trends in second-home ownership and rising costs are pushing more snowbirds to revisit whether they keep or sell Arizona homes, which can increase the number of out-of-state-led sales.   

All of this is normal here. The problem is not that you live far away. The problem is usually that nobody has a clear, coordinated plan for the property.

## How the process works when you are not in Arizona

Here is what a typical "cleanout to closing" looks like for an out-of-state Green Valley owner:

### Step 1 — Quick call and role check

We start by clarifying:

- Who is on title or who has authority (owner, PR, trustee, fiduciary).  
- Whether there is a probate, trust, or guardianship case and which attorney is handling it.  
- Your goals, timeline, and any non-negotiables (for example, a target move date, a minimum net, or a family dynamic we need to respect).

I coordinate with your attorney or fiduciary early so we all work from the same playbook.

### Step 2 — Walkthrough, photos, and a plan

I:

- Visit the home, walk it inside and out, and capture clear photos and video.  
- Note any obvious issues (roof stains, leaks, HVAC concerns, damaged flooring, yard conditions).  
- Check for HOA notices or obvious violations that could escalate if ignored.

You receive a simple summary and visuals, which you can share with your professionals. From there, I draft a step-by-step plan with phases like: secure, sort and clean out, light prep, list, and sell.

### Step 3 — Contents and vendor execution

Once you approve the plan:

- Family decision window: If needed, we give family a set period (often handled by photos, video, or one visit) to decide what must be kept.  
- Vendor estimates: I gather quotes from appropriate vendors in Green Valley for estate sale, donation, junk removal, cleaning, yard work, and basic repairs.  
- Scheduling: After you or your fiduciary approve, I schedule and oversee vendors, managing access and confirming completion.

You get short updates with photos, not a flood of texts from five different companies.

### Step 4 — Pricing, listing, and showings

With the property cleared and cleaned:

- We review comparable sales and current inventory in Green Valley's 55+ segments.   
- We choose a pricing range and positioning (as-is vs lightly improved) that fits your goals and the market.  
- I list the home on the MLS, coordinate professional photos, and manage showings.

You do not need to be present for showings. Access and security are handled via lockboxes and agent instructions.

### Step 5 — Offers, negotiation, and escrow

When offers come in:

- I summarize each offer (price, dates, contingencies, estimated net proceeds) in plain English.  
- We coordinate any needs from your attorney or fiduciary, such as particular wording when a personal representative or trustee is signing.  
- You sign documents electronically through secure e-sign tools; when notarization is required, title companies commonly arrange mail-away or remote options for out-of-state sellers in Pima County.   

During escrow:

- I manage inspections, appraisals, and access.  
- I help gather vendor receipts or summaries if your fiduciary or accountant needs them for the estate file.  
- I keep you updated so you are never guessing about next steps.

Once escrow closes, the title company disburses funds according to the instructions you, your attorney, or fiduciary have set.
