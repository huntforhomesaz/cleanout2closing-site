## How cleanout and vendor coordination works

Every property is different, but the structure can stay simple.  
Here is how I usually move from â€œwe need a lot of workâ€ to â€œthe home is ready for buyers.â€

### Step 1 â€“ Walk and map the property

We start with a property walkthrough.

On site or virtually, I look at:

- Contents: how full the home is and what types of items are present.  
- Condition: obvious repairs, safety issues, or deferred maintenance.  
- Exterior: yard, curb appeal, and any immediate concerns.

I then outline:

- What must be done for safety and basic access.  
- What should be done to present the home realistically.  
- What is optional and may or may not be worth it for your situation.

This becomes the base map for everything we do next.

### Step 2 â€“ Build a simple, written cleanout plan

From the walkthrough, I create a straightforward plan that covers:

- What you want to keep, donate, sell, or discard.  
- Which vendors are needed (cleanout crew, estate sale, donation, cleaners, yard, simple trades).  
- The recommended order of work.  
- Where legal or family decisions must come first (especially in estates, trusts, or senior moves).

You can review this plan with family, an attorney, a fiduciary, or a trusted advisor before we move ahead.

### Step 3 â€“ Gather and compare vendor estimates

Next, I line up estimates from independent vendors.

This might include:

- Junk removal and cleanout services.  
- Estate sale or auction professionals.  
- Donation and pickup services.  
- Cleaning crews.  
- Landscapers and basic handymen.  
- Trades such as plumbers, electricians, or roofers when needed.

You see estimates directly.  
We talk through pros, cons, scope, and timing in plain language.  
You decide what to approve.

### Step 4 â€“ Coordinate schedules, access, and oversight

Once you approve the work, I coordinate:

- Scheduling so vendors do not pile on the same day without reason.  
- Access via lockbox, key exchange, or scheduled meetups.  
- On-site checks at key points, such as the start of a cleanout or final walk after work is done.

You avoid constant back-and-forth calls.  
Instead, you receive simple updates and clear questions when real decisions are needed.

### Step 5 â€“ Align vendor work with your sale strategy

Cleanout and prep only make sense in context.

As your Realtor, I connect vendor work to:

- Your desired pricing and timeline.  
- Whether you plan a traditional listing, an as-is sale, or something in between.

We might decide:

- Light cleanout and yard work are enough before an as-is listing.  
- A bit more interior cleaning and small repairs will improve offers.  
- Heavier work is not worth the time or money for your situation.

I keep vendor scopes tethered to your real goals instead of chasing perfection.
