## Case study pattern 1: Probate home with out-of-state heirs

Situation:

- Tucson single-family home tied up in probate.  
- Two heirs living in different states, one named as personal representative.  
- House full of belongings, deferred maintenance, and no one local to coordinate vendors.

What we did:

- Held a three-way call with both heirs to clarify roles, timelines, and court status.  
- Walked the property on behalf of the family and documented condition with photos and video.  
- Built a simple, written plan that tied court timing to cleanout, light repairs, and listing.  
- Coordinated vendors for cleanout, basic repairs, landscaping, and cleaning.  
- Listed and sold the home as-is with clear disclosure, while keeping both heirs updated.

Outcome:

- The personal representative had organized invoices, receipts, and photos for their probate file.  
- Both heirs felt informed without having to fly in for every step.  
- The house moved from full and overwhelming to sold, with one property point of contact the whole way.

This is the kind of situation Cleanout2Closing is designed for.

## Case study pattern 3: Estate property held as a rental

Situation:

- Tucson home inherited years ago and used as a rental.  
- Wear and tear beyond normal, including long-deferred repairs.  
- Multiple decision makers and questions about how much work to do before selling.

What we did:

- Walked the property and documented condition in detail.  
- Laid out three clear paths:
  - Minimal work and an as-is sale.  
  - Moderate repairs to reach the middle of the market.  
  - Full renovation to compete with updated homes.
- Gathered estimates for each path so the family could see real numbers.  
- Coordinated the chosen level of work and managed access for vendors and tenants.  
- Listed and sold the property with pricing and marketing aligned to the chosen strategy.

Outcome:

- The family made a clear choice based on facts, not guesswork.  
- The property moved from â€œwe should do something about that houseâ€ to closed sale.  
- The personal representative had an organized record of options considered and steps taken.

Not every estate property is a time capsule.  
Some are tired rentals. The process still works.
