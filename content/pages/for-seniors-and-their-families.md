## What usually needs to happen with the house

No two homes are the same, but most senior moves follow a pattern on the property side.

Usually, you will need to:

- Decide what the senior is taking to the new place.  
- Sort what should be kept in the family, donated, sold, or let go.  
- Arrange for cleanout, cleaning, yard work, and small repairs.  
- Decide when and how to sell the home.  

Trying to do all of that alone, on top of a major life change, is where most families get overwhelmed.

My role is to:

- Help you see the steps in the right order.  
- Coordinate vendors so you are not chasing every truck and crew.  
- Guide the sale of the home as your Tucson Realtor when you are ready.

## How I help seniors and families specifically

### 1. Listen to the story and the care plan

We start with a conversation.

We talk about:

- Where the senior is now and where they may be moving.  
- Health and mobility needs that affect how fast we can work.  
- Who is involved in decisions (family, attorney, fiduciary, care team).  
- What the senior wants most from this next chapter if they are able to share that.

I do not come in with a â€œone size fits allâ€ script.  
I listen first, then we shape the property plan around the person.

### 2. Walk and document the home

Next, we walk the property.

That can look like:

- A calm, respectful walkthrough with the senior and family.  
- A quieter visit where I document the home while the senior rests or is off-site.  

I look at:

- Layout and condition of the home.  
- How full it is and what kinds of items we are dealing with.  
- Safety issues and obvious repairs.  

You receive simple photos and notes that show:

- What needs attention now.  
- What can wait.  
- What likely matters to buyers in your part of Tucson.

### 3. Build a gentle, realistic plan

From there, I draft a plan that:

- Ties the house timeline to medical appointments and move-in dates.  
- Breaks sorting and cleanout into manageable stages.  
- Identifies which vendors will be needed and when.  
- Leaves space for the emotional side of letting go.

We adjust it together so it feels realistic for both the senior and the family.

### 4. Coordinate vendors so you are not the dispatcher

With your approval, I line up:

- Movers or senior-move specialists if needed.  
- Cleanout and donation services.  
- Estate sale or auction professionals when appropriate.  
- Cleaners, landscapers, and basic trades.

You see estimates directly.  
We talk through them in plain English.  
You decide what to approve.

I coordinate schedules, access, and simple on-site checks so your days do not disappear into back-and-forth calls.

### 5. Align property work with a sale that makes sense

Not every senior home needs a full makeover.

Together we look at:

- What is the minimum work needed for safety and basic appeal.  
- Whether modest updates are likely to make a meaningful difference in the Tucson market.  
- Whether an as-is sale is more realistic for your time, energy, and budget.

### What I do not do

- I do not run junk trucks, moving crews, or estate sale events myself.

Those roles stay with:

- Medical and care providers.  
- Attorneys, fiduciaries, and financial professionals.

My focus is on the property and sale side.
