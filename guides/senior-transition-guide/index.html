Senior Transition Guide

For families helping a parent or loved one make the move to assisted living — what to expect, what to plan for, and how to handle the family home during the transition.

This Is One of the Hardest Transitions a Family Faces

When a parent can no longer safely live independently, the family faces decisions on multiple fronts simultaneously — finding the right care setting, managing the financial logistics, supporting the emotional weight of the change, and figuring out what to do with the family home.

This guide addresses the property side of that transition. For guidance on the legal and financial aspects, consult an elder law attorney and/or financial advisor.

Step 1: Understand the Housing Options

Arizona offers a range of residential care options for seniors, from independent living to memory care. Understanding the differences helps families make the right choice:

  • Independent Living Communities: For seniors who can live independently but want social engagement and convenience. Typically do not provide personal care services.
  • Assisted Living Facilities: Provide help with daily activities (bathing, dressing, medication management) while supporting independence. Licensed and regulated by the Arizona Department of Health Services.
  • Memory Care: Specialized care for residents with Alzheimer's or other forms of dementia. Secured environments with staff trained in dementia care.
  • Skilled Nursing Facilities: For residents requiring ongoing medical care and rehabilitation services.

You can verify the licensing status and complaint history of Arizona assisted living facilities at the Arizona Department of Health Services website.

Step 2: Financial Considerations

Assisted living in Arizona is primarily a private-pay expense — Medicare does not typically cover long-term assisted living costs, though it may cover some skilled nursing or rehabilitation services. Key financial considerations include:

  • Long-Term Care Insurance: If your parent has an existing policy, review it carefully for benefit triggers and coverage.
  • VA Benefits: Veterans may qualify for Aid and Attendance benefits to help cover assisted living costs.
  • Arizona ALTCS: The Arizona Long Term Care System provides Medicaid-funded long-term care for eligible low-income seniors. Consult an elder law attorney for planning and eligibility guidance.
  • Home Sale Proceeds: For many families, the sale of the family home is a primary source of funds for ongoing care costs. This is one reason the property transition process matters so much.

Step 3: Legal Preparation

Before or during the transition, ensure these legal documents are in place:

  • Durable Power of Attorney (financial)
  • Healthcare Power of Attorney / Healthcare Proxy
  • Living Will / Advance Directive
  • Current Will or Trust documents
  • POLST (Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment) if appropriate

An Arizona elder law attorney can help establish or update these documents appropriately.

Step 4: Managing the Family Home

Once the decision is made to move to assisted living, the family home becomes an asset that needs to be managed — and eventually, usually sold. This is where many families feel most overwhelmed.

The property needs to be cleared of personal belongings, assessed for needed repairs, maintained during the transition, and ultimately prepared for sale. For out-of-state family members, coordinating all of this from a distance adds significant complexity.
